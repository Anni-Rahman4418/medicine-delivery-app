# Information Gathering

## Introduction

Information gathering is an important phase in system development. It helps to understand user needs, system requirements, and real-world challenges before designing the solution.

---

## Methods Used for Information Gathering

### 1. Online Research

Information was collected from:

* Existing medicine delivery applications
* Healthcare and e-commerce systems
* Articles about digital healthcare solutions

This helped in understanding common features such as:

* Online ordering systems
* Prescription upload systems
* Delivery tracking mechanisms

---

### 2. Observation

The current process of buying medicines from local pharmacies was observed. It was found that:

* Users must physically visit pharmacies
* Long waiting times are common
* Emergency medicine access is difficult

---

### 3. User Needs Analysis

Basic user needs were identified based on general healthcare requirements:

* Easy access to medicines
* Fast and reliable delivery
* Simple mobile interface
* Secure prescription handling

---

### 4. Informal Discussions

Discussions with potential users (patients and pharmacy staff) indicated:

* Demand for home delivery of medicines
* Need for prescription-based ordering
* Importance of order tracking

---

## Key Findings

* There is a strong need for a digital medicine delivery platform
* Users prefer simple and fast mobile applications
* Pharmacies need better inventory and order management systems
* Prescription verification is essential for safety

---

## Conclusion

The information gathered confirms the need for a centralized medicine delivery system. These findings were used to design the features and structure of the MediGo application.
